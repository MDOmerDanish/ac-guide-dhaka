## 🧮 2. Tonnage (BTU) Capacity Calculation

To ensure energy efficiency and durable operation, the heat load is calculated as follows:

1.  **Base Cooling Capacity (Area-based):** 250 sq ft × 80 BTU/sq ft = **20,000 BTU/hr**
2.  **Top Floor Multiplier (+15%):** +3,000 BTU/hr *(massive radiant heat from the concrete roof slab)*
3.  **High Ceiling Multiplier (+10%):** +2,000 BTU/hr *(10 ft ceiling increases the total air volume)*
4.  **Windows & Doors Multiplier:** +2,000 BTU/hr *(2 windows + 1 door thermal leakage)*
5.  **Occupancy Heat Load:** +600 BTU/hr *(1 person)*

*   **Total Calculated Heat Load:** **≈ 27,600 BTU/hr**

### 🏷️ Recommended AC Size: **2.5 Ton (30,000 BTU/hr)** or a heavy-duty **2.0 Ton (24,000 BTU/hr)**

*   **Why 1.5 Ton (18,000 BTU) is too small:** It will fail to cool the room during peak summer afternoons. The compressor will run continuously at 100% capacity, resulting in extremely high electricity bills and premature wear.
*   **Why 2.0 Ton (24,000 BTU) is risky but possible:** A standard 2.0 Ton might struggle during extreme Dhaka summer heatwaves (38°C–42°C) on a top floor. If you buy a 2.0 Ton, it *must* be a heavy-duty, high-EER model with active inverter cooling.
*   **Why 2.5 Ton (30,000 BTU) is the Sweet Spot:** It provides a safe headroom. With an **Inverter**, this extra power allows the AC to cool the room down to your set temperature in just a few minutes, after which the compressor slows down to a low-power state, saving 50% to 60% electricity.
